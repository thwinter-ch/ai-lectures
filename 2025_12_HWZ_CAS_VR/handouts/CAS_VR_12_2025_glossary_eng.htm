A
API (Application Programming Interface)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A digital messenger that allows two different software programs to talk to each other and exchange data.
The Analogy
Think of a waiter in a restaurant. You (the user) don't go into the kitchen (the server) to cook. You hand your order to the waiter, they take it to the kitchen, and bring the food back to you.
Why It Matters
APIs are the "glue" of modern business; without them, your expensive software silos cannot share data or automate workflows.
Augmented Reality (AR)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
Technology that overlays digital information (like images or text) onto the real world you see through a screen or glasses.
The Analogy
Like a car's Heads-Up Display (HUD) projecting your speed onto the windshield so you don't have to look down at the dashboard.
Why It Matters
Critical for future field service efficiency (e.g., a technician seeing repair manuals overlaid on a broken machine).
B
Blockchain
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A shared digital ledger where transactions are recorded permanently and cannot be deleted or altered by anyone.
The Analogy
Imagine a Google Doc shared with the whole world where everyone can view it, anyone can add a line, but absolutely no one—not even the creator—can delete or edit a line once it's written.
Why It Matters
It removes the need for expensive "middlemen" (intermediaries) to establish trust in transactions.
C
Cloud Act
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A US law that allows US authorities to demand data from American tech companies (like Microsoft/Google), even if that data is stored on servers in Switzerland.
The Analogy
Like a parent telling their child, "I don't care that you hid your diary at your friend's house; you live under my roof, so you have to go get it for me."
Why It Matters
Storing data in a "Swiss Data Center" owned by a US company does not guarantee total legal immunity from US jurisdiction.
Context Window
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
The amount of information (short-term memory) an AI can hold in its "head" during a single conversation before it forgets the beginning.
The Analogy
Like a whiteboard in a meeting. Once the board is full, you have to erase the top to write new ideas, losing the earlier context.
Why It Matters
If your documents are larger than the window, the AI cannot analyze the whole thing at once.
D
Data Sovereignty
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
The concept that data is subject to the laws and governance structures within the nation it is collected.
The Analogy
Having a safe in your own house (Sovereignty) vs. renting a locker in a bank owned by a foreign country (Residency).
Why It Matters
Crucial for risk management; "Data Residency" (location) is not the same as "Sovereignty" (legal control).
Digital Twin
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A virtual replica of a physical object (like a machine) or process that lets you run tests without risking the real asset.
The Analogy
Like a flight simulator for a specific plane; you can crash the simulator 100 times to learn, without scratching the real aircraft.
Why It Matters
Allows companies to predict maintenance and optimize production lines without expensive downtime.
G
Generative AI (GenAI)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
AI that can create *new* content (text, images, code) rather than just analyzing existing data.
The Analogy
Traditional AI is a librarian who finds a book for you. Generative AI is a ghostwriter who writes a new book for you.
Why It Matters
It democratizes creativity and drastically lowers the cost of producing drafts, code, and marketing assets.
H
Hallucination
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
When an AI confidently presents false or made-up information as fact.
The Analogy
Like a very confident person at a bar who doesn't know the answer but invents a believable story so they don't look stupid.
Why It Matters
This is a major liability risk; AI output must be verified, not blindly trusted.
Hype Cycle (Gartner)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A graphic curve that predicts how technologies go from "Over-Enthusiasm" to "Deep Disappointment" before finally becoming useful.
The Analogy
Like a teenage romance: Infatuation (Peak of Expectations), the Breakup (Trough of Disillusionment), and finally a mature friendship (Plateau of Productivity).
Why It Matters
Helps the Board decide *when* to invest—too early creates waste, too late means missing the boat.
I
Inference
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
The moment the AI actually "thinks" and answers your specific question using its training.
The Analogy
"Training" is the student studying for 4 years. "Inference" is the student actually sitting down to take the exam.
Why It Matters
When you use AI, you aren't "training" it; you are running inference. Data entered here (usually) doesn't teach the model new facts permanently unless specified.
L
Large Language Model (LLM)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A type of AI trained on vast amounts of text to understand and generate human-like language (e.g., GPT-4).
The Analogy
Imagine a parrot that has read every book in the world and is incredibly good at predicting which word should come next in a sentence.
Why It Matters
These are the engines powering the current AI revolution; they are general-purpose reasoning engines.
Legacy System
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
Old software or hardware that is still essential for operations but difficult to update or integrate with modern tools.
The Analogy
The foundation of a house built in 1950. It holds the house up, but if you try to drill into it to add new wiring, the whole thing might crack.
Why It Matters
These are major security vulnerabilities (hard to patch) and innovation blockers.
O
One-Way Door Decision
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A strategic decision that is nearly impossible or extremely expensive to reverse once made.
The Analogy
Quitting your job (One-Way) vs. trying a new restaurant for lunch (Two-Way).
Why It Matters
Boards should spend 80% of their energy on One-Way Doors (e.g., major ERP migration) and delegate Two-Way Doors.
P
Prompt Engineering
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
The art of writing precise instructions to get the best possible result out of an AI.
The Analogy
It's the difference between Googling "pasta" vs. "authentic carbonara recipe without cream." The quality of the question dictates the quality of the answer.
Why It Matters
Employees need training on this; a standardized "Prompt Library" increases organizational productivity.
Q
Quantum Computing
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A new type of computer based on physics that solves simulation problems (like chemistry or logistics) exponentially faster than current computers.
The Analogy
A classic computer tries to find the way out of a maze by walking every path one by one. A quantum computer walks all paths simultaneously.
Why It Matters
It will eventually break current encryption standards and revolutionize drug discovery and logistics.
R
RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A technique where you link an AI to your own private company data so it answers questions accurately based on *your* facts.
The Analogy
Taking a test with an open textbook. You don't have to memorize the facts; you just need to know how to read the book (your data) to find the answer.
Why It Matters
This is the standard for Enterprise AI—it reduces hallucinations and keeps data private/relevant.
Ransomware
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
Malware that locks up your company's data and demands payment to release it.
The Analogy
Someone changing the locks on your house while you are outside, then charging you $50,000 for the new key.
Why It Matters
It is a business continuity risk, not just an IT problem. Paying doesn't guarantee data recovery.
S
Shadow AI
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
When employees use unauthorized AI tools (like free ChatGPT) for work without IT's knowledge.
The Analogy
Employees bringing their own power tools to a construction site because the company tools are too slow—it gets the job done but creates massive safety risks.
Why It Matters
Leads to data leakage (e.g., pasting strategy documents into public AI models).
Smart Contract
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A self-executing digital contract where the terms are written into code—if X happens, Y executes automatically.
The Analogy
A vending machine. You don't need a lawyer to verify the transaction. You put money in, select item, and the machine automatically releases the product.
Why It Matters
Automates compliance and payments, reducing legal disputes and administrative overhead.
Social Engineering
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
Hacking the *human*, not the machine, by manipulating people into giving up passwords or money.
The Analogy
The "Prince of Nigeria" email scam, or a fake CEO calling finance to request an "urgent wire transfer."
Why It Matters
Humans are the "weakest link" in cybersecurity; technical firewalls cannot stop a tricked employee.
T
Token (AI Context)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
The basic unit of text that an AI processes; roughly equivalent to 3/4 of a word.
The Analogy
Think of syllables. You pay the AI provider per syllable generated, not per request.
Why It Matters
This is the cost basis for AI. "Cost per token" is the key metric for scaling AI operations.
Token (Blockchain/Crypto)
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A digital asset representing ownership or value (money, a vote, a share) on a blockchain.
The Analogy
A casino chip. It represents value within the casino (the blockchain ecosystem) that can be exchanged or cashed out.
Why It Matters
Allows for the digitization of real-world assets like company shares (e.g., Daura) or real estate.
Z
Zero Trust
The "Cocktail Party" Definition
A security concept where no user or device is trusted by default, even if they are already inside the office network.
The Analogy
Most offices have a badge reader at the front door (perimeter security). Zero Trust puts a badge reader on every single door inside the building, including the bathroom.
Why It Matters
The only effective defense against modern ransomware, assuming hackers *will* eventually get past the firewall.
