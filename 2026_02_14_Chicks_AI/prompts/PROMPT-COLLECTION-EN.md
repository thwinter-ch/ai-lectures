## 1. Professional Profile Picture

**What:** Create a professional photo for LinkedIn or your website
**Tool:** Gemini (free)

```
Create a professional portrait photo for a LinkedIn profile.

Person: [YOUR DESCRIPTION: Gender, approximate age, hair color/length, skin tone]
Style: Professional, friendly, approachable
Clothing: [e.g., dark blouse, white shirt, business attire]
Background: Neutral light gray, slightly blurred
Lighting: Soft studio light from the front
Eye contact: Looking directly at the camera, friendly smile
Framing: Head and shoulders, some space above the head

No logos, no text, no watermarks.
Photorealistic, 8K quality.
```

**Example filled in:**
```
Create a professional portrait photo for a LinkedIn profile.

Person: Woman, early 50s, shoulder-length blonde hair, European skin tone
Style: Professional, friendly, approachable
Clothing: Turquoise blouse, business casual
Background: Neutral light gray, slightly blurred
Lighting: Soft studio light from the front
Eye contact: Looking directly at the camera, warm smile
Framing: Head and shoulders, some space above the head

No logos, no text, no watermarks.
Photorealistic, 8K quality.
```

**Tips:**
- The more specific your description, the better the result
- Try multiple versions (generate 2-3 images)
- If you don't like something, say it: "Make the lighting softer" or "Change the background to dark blue"

## 2. Interview Setting / Avatar Image

**What:** Create a cinematic image, like in a professional interview or talk show
**Tool:** Gemini (free)

```
Create an ultra-realistic cinematic photo.

Person: [YOUR DESCRIPTION: as above]
Setting: Professional studio interview, like a Netflix documentary
Position: Sitting on a comfortable sofa or modern chair, slightly turned toward the camera
Clothing: [e.g., business casual, elegant blouse, shirt]
Background: Modern studio with warm lighting, slightly blurred
Lights: Warm studio spotlights from above, soft shadows
Mood: Relaxed, professional, in conversation
Camera: 9:16 format (portrait), shallow depth of field

Photorealistic, 8K quality, premium editorial style.
```

**Example:**
```
Create an ultra-realistic cinematic photo.

Person: Woman, mid-40s, short brown hair, European skin tone
Setting: Professional studio interview, like a Netflix documentary
Position: Sitting on a beige sofa, slightly turned toward the camera, hands relaxed on lap
Clothing: Elegant dark green blouse
Background: Modern studio with plants and warm light, slightly blurred
Lights: Warm studio spotlights from above, soft shadows
Mood: Relaxed, professional, slight smile during conversation
Camera: 9:16 format (portrait), shallow depth of field

Photorealistic, 8K quality, premium editorial style.
```

**Tips:**
- This setting looks more dynamic than a classic portrait
- Great for "About Me" pages or social media
- You can add props: "A coffee cup on the table" or "A book in hand"

## 3. YouTube Video Summary

**What:** Summarize a YouTube video in 3 key points ‚Äî practical and actionable
**Tool:** Gemini (free, with YouTube link)

```
Watch this YouTube video: [YOUTUBE LINK HERE]

Please summarize it:

1. **The 3 key insights** ‚Äî what are the main points?
2. **What can I do with this?** ‚Äî practical application
3. **The one sentence** ‚Äî what's the most important takeaway I should remember?

Keep it short and practical. No long explanations.
```

**Example:**
```
Watch this YouTube video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dQw4w9WgXcQ

Please summarize it:

1. **The 3 key insights** ‚Äî what are the main points?
2. **What can I do with this?** ‚Äî practical application
3. **The one sentence** ‚Äî what's the most important takeaway I should remember?

Keep it short and practical. No long explanations.
```

**Tips:**
- Works with any public YouTube video
- Gemini watches the video and analyzes it
- Great for long tutorials, interviews, or conference talks
- You can follow up: "Explain point 2 in more detail" or "Are there more practical tips?"

**Pro version:**
```
Watch this YouTube video: [LINK]

Summarize for someone in [YOUR INDUSTRY/ROLE]:
1. The 3 most important points
2. Concrete implementation in my job
3. One sentence to remember

Practical, short, no fluff.
```

## Bonus Tips for Better Prompts

### ‚úÖ Do this:
- Be specific: "Dark blue blouse" instead of "nice clothing"
- Give context: "For my LinkedIn profile as a nutritionist"
- Iterate: If the result doesn't fit ‚Üí say what you want to change
- Save good prompts: In your Second Brain or in a note

### ‚ùå Avoid:
- Vague instructions: "Make something nice"
- Too many details at once (AI gets confused)
- Sharing secrets: Passwords, confidential info don't belong in prompts
