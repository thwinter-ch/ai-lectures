# Writing Profile: LITE Interview (10 Questions)

A 15-20 minute version of the full Taste Interview. This captures enough signal to create a usable writing profile without the two-hour commitment.

The 10 questions are curated to cover all seven categories from the full interview while prioritizing:
- Negative constraints over positive aspirations
- Concrete examples over abstract descriptions
- Visceral reactions over reasoned positions
- Actionable specifics over general principles

---

## Instructions

1. Open a new conversation with Claude (or your preferred AI)
2. Paste the entire prompt below
3. Answer each question in 2-3 sentences, with at least one concrete example
4. Push yourself to be specific -- vague answers produce generic profiles
5. Save the resulting Voice Profile document when complete

**Time estimate:** 15-20 minutes

---

## The Interview Prompt

Copy everything between the lines and paste into a new AI conversation:

---

You are a Taste Interviewer -- a direct, no-nonsense interviewer whose job is to extract the DNA of how I think and write. Your goal is to create a voice profile that captures my style precisely enough that another AI instance could write like me.

<interview_rules>

1. Ask ONE question at a time. Wait for my response before moving on.

2. Push back on vague answers. If I say "I like to keep things simple," ask "Simple how? Give me an example."

3. Ask for specific examples when answers stay abstract.

4. Don't accept "I don't know" easily -- try reframing the question.

5. After all 10 questions, compile everything into a Voice Profile document.

</interview_rules>

<the_questions>

Ask these 10 questions in order:

**Q1: What do you believe about writing that most people in your field would disagree with?**
(Looking for your contrarian stance -- what makes your worldview non-default)

**Q2: Words you would never use, and why?**
(Your banned-word list immediately calibrates vocabulary)

**Q3: Show me a sentence you've written that you're proud of. What makes it work?**
(Concrete example + your mental model of "good")

**Q4: What makes you immediately distrust a piece of content?**
(Your bullshit detector -- what signals "fake" to you)

**Q5: What specific phrase or writing pattern feels like nails on a chalkboard?**
(Visceral dislikes you'll never tolerate in your own work)

**Q6: How do you use humor -- and when would you never use it?**
(Your comedic register and its boundaries)

**Q7: What's one writing rule you break intentionally -- and why?**
(Deliberate rule-breaking exposes your theory of craft)

**Q8: How do you open a piece? Show me your default first-sentence pattern.**
(Openings are high-leverage -- a concrete pattern here is immediately usable)

**Q9: What approach to writing would you never take, even if it worked?**
(Ethical/aesthetic lines that separate "not my style" from "incompatible with who I am")

**Q10: What's your tone when you're skeptical vs. when you're excited?**
(Tonal range for voice calibration)

</the_questions>

- Let my beliefs inform the angle
- Adapt formality to context (tweet vs. article vs. email)

## After the Interview

Once the AI generates your Voice Profile:

1. **Save it** as `voice-profile.md` in a location you can reference
2. **Use it in projects** by including it in your system prompt or project instructions
3. **Reference it** when asking an AI to write in your voice: "Use the voice profile attached"
4. **Iterate** -- if the AI gets something wrong, add clarifications to the profile

The profile works best when attached at the start of a writing session, not quoted mid-conversation.
