# Technology Revolution — Speaker Script

**Segment:** 02 | **Duration:** 15 minutes | **Position:** After "How I Built This"
**Source:** Nate B Jones "The Skill Gap That Will Separate AI Winners from Everyone Else"

---

## Opening Hook [0:00–1:30]

> "Show of hands — who has tried to delegate a multi-step task to an AI assistant... and had it go sideways?"
>
> [Pause for hands]
>
> "Now here's the uncomfortable truth: the AI probably wasn't the problem."

**Key point:** 2026 is the year the technical barriers fell. The new bottleneck is us.

---

## The Year Everything Changed [1:30–4:00]

Walk through the 2026 breakthrough stack:

| Capability | What Changed | Why It Matters |
|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Hardware cycle** | Consumer devices finally AI-ready | Agents can run always-on, locally |
| **Sustained attention** | Agents maintain focus for hours | No more "let me think about that..." 3 seconds later: nothing |
| **Memory scaffolding** | Persistent context across sessions | The "amnesiac agent" problem is solved |
| **MCP + Skills** | Model Context Protocol standardizes tools | AI can actually do things, not just say things |
| **Browser/file use** | Autonomous computer interaction | Research, write, file, repeat — hands-off |

**Emphasis:** "This isn't a 2030 roadmap. This is what shipped. The pieces are assembled."

---

## The Missing Piece [4:00–6:30]

**Core thesis:** All technical components exist. What's missing is the interface layer — and the human skill to use it.

**The problem isn't the engine:**

```
What We Have:              What We're Missing:
- MCP                      - Intuitive UX (the always-on right pane)
- Browser automation       - Task formulation skill
- File manipulation        - Intentional delegation muscle
- Memory persistence       - Clear handoff protocols
- Hours of sustained work  - Organized systems to delegate INTO
```

**Provocative framing:** "The bottleneck moved. It used to be 'can AI do this?' Now it's 'can YOU define what needs to be done clearly enough to delegate it?'"

---

## The Two-Part Architecture [6:30–9:00]

**Explain the agent architecture that works:**

### Layer 1: Translation Layer
- Converts messy human intent into structured tasks
- Handles ambiguity, asks clarifying questions
- This is where YOU interact

### Layer 2: Task Execution Layer
- Receives clean, decomposed instructions
- Executes reliably, reports back
- This is where agents shine

**Key insight:** "LLM work product is finally good enough to trust. The output quality crossed the threshold. But the translation layer — the interface between your brain and the agent — that's still being built. And until it exists, YOU are the translation layer."

**Rhetorical beat:** "Which means your skill at translating vague intentions into executable tasks is now a competitive advantage."

---

## The Mini-Me Question [9:00–11:00]

**The race that matters:**

> "Who will build your mini-me first?"

Three contenders:
1. **You** — deliberately training your AI on how you think and work
2. **Your competitor** — who figured this out faster
3. **Nobody** — and you stay in the 'type everything manually' lane

**The advantage of going first:**
- Your AI accumulates context about you
- Delegation gets easier, not harder
- Compound returns on every interaction

**The cost of waiting:**
- Others build institutional knowledge in AI while you don't
- The gap widens monthly
- Playing catch-up in 2027 is significantly harder

## What This Means for Financial Services [11:00–13:00]

**Specific applications for this room:**

| Domain | What Agents Can Do Now | What You Need to Define |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Due diligence** | Research 20 sources, synthesize findings | What constitutes a red flag for YOUR deals |
| **Client prep** | Build briefing docs from multiple sources | Your format, your emphasis, your question framework |
| **Market monitoring** | Track signals across sources continuously | What signals matter to YOUR strategy |
| **Report generation** | Draft quarterly reviews, board materials | Your voice, your structure, your story arc |

**Key line:** "The AI can execute. But it can't know what matters to you unless you've externalized it."
